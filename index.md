#### Project A: Personalized Value-Aligned Language Models
In this project, I am building and evaluating language models that adapt their behavior to an individual user‚Äôs personal values, as measured by Schwartz‚Äôs value survey and related scenario-based instruments. We focus on combining psychometric value measurements (PVQ-style items and social dilemma scenarios) with prompt engineering and activation steering techniques to condition model outputs on a user‚Äôs value profile. The goal is to understand when and how aligning models to users‚Äô values improves perceived fit, trust, and usefulness without sacrificing safety.

We use methods from representation learning and mechanistic interpretability (e.g., behavior vectors and activation steering), along with web-based data collection (React frontend, Supabase backend) to gather value scores, choices in carefully designed dilemmas, and preferences over model responses. My specific contributions include designing the value-elicitation scenarios, implementing the survey and interaction UI, integrating activation steering into the model pipeline, and analyzing preference data to compare aligned vs. original vs. misaligned model behavior. This work has applications in personal and professional AI assistants, decision support tools, and educational or coaching systems where respecting a user‚Äôs values is critical for long-term, trustworthy collaboration.
